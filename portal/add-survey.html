Contribute a survey

Files first. Drop your EDI or MTH5 transfer functions, watch the stations land on the map, then fill the short required section below. The optional sections help us publish faster, but you can skip straight to package and submit. Everything runs in your browser; your files and details never leave this page until you submit. New here? A worked example survey package lives at engine/data/sample-survey/ in the AusMT framework repository.

This page performs local pre-submission checks to help you prepare a clean submission. Final validation is authoritative in the AusMT submission pipeline, the gateway runs the AusMT validator on your package and a curator reviews it before publication; if the two ever differ, the pipeline result wins.

1. Your files

start here
Drop .edi or .mth5 / .h5 files here, or click to choose. Files stay in your browser.
MTH5 files containing transfer-function products are supported. AusMT catalogues transfer functions and their metadata, not raw MT time-series data. Files are checked locally now; the AusMT validator in the submission pipeline is authoritative during curator review.

2. The essentials

required

Six fields and two tick-boxes. This is everything the AusMT validator requires.

Folder name (auto): au.
Every access level lists this survey publicly, its map presence, exact station coordinates and screening diagnostics are visible to anyone regardless of level. open also serves the data files for download. metadata_only and embargoed withhold all data bytes (downloads route to the source archive) until a curator releases them; an embargo never lifts automatically, even after its date passes.
The intended release date. Recommended for an embargoed survey. Even after this date the data stays withheld until a curator sets the level to open, it is a record of intent, not an automatic switch.
An email or role address a curator can reach to arrange release. Written into survey.yaml, do not use a private personal address you would not want published.
Defaults to Australia. Edit it if your survey is elsewhere.
Private, for curator contact only. Not written into the submission package, the gateway sends it to the curators privately, or include it in your email to the operator.

4. I know my metadata

advanced

Provenance identifiers, instruments, processing and data types. Leave these to the curator if you are unsure, server validation and curator review fill any gaps.

Start by picking what the identifier points at; the citation relation is set for you. The raw time-series collection at NCI is Raw time series; a GA eCAT or state landing page is Entire dataset; a paper goes under Related publication above, not here.

Package semver: 1.0.1 metadata fix, 1.1.0 added stations, 2.0.0 reprocessed transfer functions.

5. Check and package

What happens next: package your submission here, then either upload it directly with a submit key (when a gateway is available on this site) or email the packaged .zip to the AusMT operator (contact on the About page). The pipeline runs a ClamAV virus scan and the AusMT validator, a curator reviews it, and the survey is published. The local checks here are advisory; server validation is authoritative.
