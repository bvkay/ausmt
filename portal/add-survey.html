Contribute a survey

Describe the survey, add your EDI files, then validate, preview, and package a submission archive. Submit it directly to AusMT where a gateway is available (no account needed); otherwise email the packaged archive to the operator — the contact address is on the About page. Everything runs in your browser; your files and details never leave this page until you submit. New here? A worked example survey package lives at engine/data/sample-survey/ in the AusMT framework repository.

This page performs local pre-submission checks to help you prepare a clean submission. Final validation is authoritative in the AusMT repository workflow (CI) during curator review, if the two ever differ, the repository result wins.

Every access level lists this survey publicly — its map presence, exact station coordinates and screening diagnostics are visible to anyone regardless of level. open also serves the data files for download. metadata_only and embargoed withhold all data bytes (downloads route to the source archive) until a curator releases them; an embargo never lifts automatically, even after its date passes.
The intended release date. Recommended for an embargoed survey. Even after this date the data stays withheld until a curator sets the level to open — it is a record of intent, not an automatic switch.
An email or role address a curator can reach to arrange release. Written into survey.yaml — do not use a private personal address you would not want published.
Surveys sharing a collection are grouped in the portal and MTCAT. Collections describe programmes or campaigns (for example, AusLAMP) and hold no transfer functions themselves, all data, metadata, and provenance remain with the individual surveys. Use the same collection identifier and title across all member surveys.
Transfer functions
Drop .edi or .mth5 / .h5 files here, or click to choose. Files stay in your browser.
MTH5 files containing transfer-function products are supported. AusMT catalogues transfer functions and their metadata, not raw MT time-series data. Files are checked locally before submission, with final validation performed during curator review and automated repository checks.

Plots every station from your EDI HEAD coordinates. Some instruments write a corrupted HEAD coordinate (a DMS sign/floor bug) while the correct decimal survives in the EDI INFO block — those stations are shown with both candidates so you can choose the right one.
