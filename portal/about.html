What is AusMT?

AusMT is a portal for Australian magnetotelluric (MT) transfer functions, the processed, instrument-independent response of the Earth at each site. It is a discovery and provenance layer, not a raw-data archive. In one place it provides:

  • Discovery - find MT stations and surveys across the country on a map and by metadata.
  • Preservation - keep processed EDI/MTH5 products and their metadata together and citable.
  • Provenance - record exactly how each transfer function was produced and from which file.
  • Citation - generate ready-to-use citations and acknowledgements crediting the custodians.
  • Contribution - let researchers add their own surveys through a guided, reviewed workflow.

Principles

Five principles guide what AusMT builds and the decisions its curators make:

  • Discoverable - every survey and station is findable in one place, on a map and by metadata, regardless of which institution produced it.
  • Reusable - products are described with consistent, standards-aligned metadata and explicit licences so others can confidently reuse them.
  • Transparent - how each transfer function was produced is recorded and shown; unknown values are marked "not recorded" rather than guessed.
  • Attributable - custodians, investigators, and funders are credited, with DOIs, ORCIDs, RORs, and RAiDs carried through to discovery.
  • Reproducible - products come from a single, versioned build pipeline whose provenance travels with the data, so results can be regenerated and checked.

Why was AusMT created?

Australian MT data is scattered. The same scientific results are spread across university groups, government agencies, industry collaborations, supplementary files attached to papers, and ageing legacy archives, all in a mix of formats and conventions. That makes good data hard to find and easy to lose.

AusMT improves discoverability (one searchable map and catalogue), reproducibility (provenance travels with every product), reuse (consistent, standards-aligned metadata), and attribution (custodians and funders are credited, with DOIs where they exist).

Scope

Included

  • Transfer functions
  • EDI products
  • MTH5 products
  • Survey metadata
  • Station metadata
  • Publications & DOIs

Not included

  • Raw time-series
  • Geological interpretation
  • Commercially restricted datasets

Where raw time-series exist, AusMT links to the archive that holds them (for example the NCI-AuScope MT collection) rather than hosting them itself.

Provenance

Every station record carries a processing-provenance panel so a result can be traced and trusted. For each transfer function AusMT records the source file, its SHA-256 checksum, the extractor used (mt_metadata — the community-canonical engine), the pipeline software version, the build version, and the git commit of the build. When a value genuinely isn't known it is shown as "not recorded" rather than guessed.

Data policy

  • Custodians retain ownership. Contributing to AusMT does not transfer any rights.
  • AusMT does not claim ownership of any dataset it indexes or redistributes.
  • Redistribution depends on the licence. Openly licensed transfer functions may be served directly; otherwise AusMT lists the record and links out to the custodian's archive.
  • Some products are linked, not hosted, discovery is universal even where hosting isn't.

Contributing data

You can prepare a submission entirely in your browser, your files never leave your machine, and the workflow mirrors the curator's command-line tools:

You validate locally, preview what the portal will show, and package a submission archive. A curator then reviews it and decides on publication — local checks are advisory, repository review is authoritative.

Standards

AusMT is built to align with established research-data and MT-domain standards:

DOI — dataset & publication identifiers ORCID — researcher identifiers ROR — organisation identifiers RAiD — project identifiers FAIR — Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable CARE — Indigenous data governance principles mt_metadata — community MT metadata MTH5 — community MT data container

For interoperability between portals, AusMT emits an MTCAT discovery document (see the MTCAT v1.0 specification and FEDERATION.md in the project repositories).

Governance

AusMT is maintained as research infrastructure, not a commercial service. Surveys are published through a curator review process: a contributor prepares a submission, automated repository checks run, and a maintainer reviews metadata and licensing before anything is published. Publication decisions rest with the curators. AusMT is designed to sit alongside national infrastructure such as AuScope and the MT community (e.g. AuScope/ANSIR instrument programs), and to migrate to a long-term institutional home as it matures.

Who runs AusMT

AusMT is operated by Ben Kay, National Geophysics Program Manager, AuScope, as a pre-institutional deployment of the AusMT framework, on a path to hosting within AuScope/NCI infrastructure.

Contact, corrections and takedown — questions, correction requests, or takedown requests: ben@auscope.org.au. Requests are acknowledged within five business days. Takedown requests for contested data are actioned as a priority: the data is withheld from distribution while the matter is resolved with the originating custodian.

Data responsibility — survey data remains the property and responsibility of its originating custodians under their stated licence. AusMT redistributes transfer-function files only where the licence and access level permit; otherwise it lists metadata with a pointer to the source archive. Responsibility for the scientific accuracy of contributed data rests with the originating custodian; AusMT records and preserves provenance but does not alter scientific content.

Succession — the AusMT framework is open source (Apache-2.0) and fully documented; the catalogue and all served packages are rebuildable from the survey source repository by any operator, so no data or capability is locked to the current operator.

Future directions

  • Validation of AusMT against a broader range of real-world surveys, metadata records, and externally authored MTH5 datasets.
  • Expansion of the Australian MT catalogue through the addition of historical, legacy, and newly acquired surveys.
  • Evolution of the MTCAT JSON schema and discovery API as an open standard for interoperable MT catalogue discovery and federation.
  • Support for federation between independent MT portals while preserving local stewardship, provenance, and ownership of data holdings.
AusMT is a national MT research infrastructure. It indexes processed transfer functions and their provenance; it does not host raw time-series or make geological interpretations.
